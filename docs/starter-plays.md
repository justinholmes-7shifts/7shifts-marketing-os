# Starter plays

Setup gets you a loaded brain. Impact comes from what you do with it. These are the plays that pay off in your first week.

The thing that makes them fast: because the brain is cloned and your CLAUDE.md points at it, you don't re-explain who we sell to, how we sound, or what we look like. You just ask, and the answer comes back on-brand and grounded in our real ICP, personas, and positioning. Paste a prompt, react to the output.

---

### 1. Brief a campaign, already pressure-tested

**When:** you've got a campaign idea and need a brief that survives a leadership conversation.

**Say:** *"I want to run [idea] for [segment, e.g. 30+ employee FSRs]. Pressure-test it with the lifecycle-campaign-strategy skill, then write the brief."*

**You get:** a brief that names the customer outcome, the one business metric, the audience state, and the kill condition, built on our real ICP and personas. Fifteen minutes instead of a day, and it's defensible.

### 2. Write copy that leads with the number, in our voice

**When:** any email, landing module, or ad.

**Say:** *"Draft the email for [campaign] aimed at [persona, e.g. Michelle, the high-volume FSR owner]. Use our voice and the value-prop standard."*

**You get:** copy that leads with the operator's outcome and a real number (14 hours, $1,045 in overtime), sounds like 7shifts, and has zero banned words. A usable first draft, not a starting-from-blank one.

### 3. Get an on-brand asset without a design round-trip

**When:** you need a hero, a slide, or a social post mocked up.

**Say:** *"Design a web hero for [campaign]. On brand."*

**You get:** a mockup with the right colors, type, and bento layout, because the brand-designer skill knows the 7shifts system. Good enough to react to instead of waiting on a v1.

### 4. Walk into a segment conversation ready

**When:** prepping for a call, a webinar, or sales enablement.

**Say:** *"Give me the talking points for [persona]: their top fears, what they care about, the objections they'll raise, and the proof points that land."*

**You get:** a tight brief built from our personas and proof points, so you sound like you live in their world.

### 5. Pressure-test a plan before it eats capacity

**When:** someone proposes a campaign, or you're about to commit to one.

**Say:** *"Run this through the pressure test."* (paste the plan)

**You get:** the 11 hard questions answered or flagged, the weak spots named, and a redirect if it's not ready. Saves you from building the wrong thing.

### 6. Turn one asset into a week of channels

**When:** you've got a blog post, a webinar, or a case study.

**Say:** *"Repurpose this into a launch email, three LinkedIn posts, and an in-app modal."* (point to the source)

**You get:** channel-right versions (the framework knows our program types and channels), all in voice.

### 7. Tighten anything before it ships

**When:** any draft, Slack post, or doc.

**Say:** *"Tighten this to our voice, kill the banned words, make it clearer."*

**You get:** a shorter, on-voice version, with the clear-communications skill checking the structure.

### 8. Bank what worked

**When:** a campaign wraps or a test gives you a result.

**Say:** *"Share this learning: [what happened]."*

**You get:** it written into the brain and pushed, so the next person inherits it. This is the play that makes the team compound, not just move faster individually. Pull from the brain, do the work, push the learning back.

---

Pick one this week. The first time a brief or a piece of copy comes back already on-brand and already grounded in our segments, you'll get why the brain is worth keeping current.
