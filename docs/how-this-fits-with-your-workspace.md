# How this fits with your Claude Code setup

Two things are landing with the team this week, and I want to make sure they read as one move instead of two competing ones, because they stack.

Tamara's "Claude Code Setup" doc gets your own workspace running: the folder structure, your personal CLAUDE.md, your memory, and the session habits that keep the whole thing from turning into a junk drawer by month two. That's yours. It's how Claude learns who you are and what you're actually in the middle of.

The 7shifts Marketing OS is the shared brain that everyone pulls into that workspace: our brand guidelines, the ICP and the segment sizing, positioning, the seven personas, the voice rules, and a growing set of skills (a brand designer, the lifecycle and campaign strategy thinking layer, clear communications). One repo, everybody clones it, everybody works from the same copy.

Here's why it's two things and not one. If each of us drops our own copy of the brand guidelines and the PMM value props into our own folder, we end up with eight versions of the truth, and the day brand updates the color palette, seven of them are quietly wrong. The shared brain fixes that at the root. Update the voice file once and every person's next piece of work uses the new version. The brain also gets smarter every time someone feeds a learning back into it, which a pile of private folders never does.

The restaurant version: Tamara's doc is how you set up your own station. The Marketing OS is the walk-in the whole kitchen shares. You don't each go buy your own tomatoes.

What to do, in order:

1. Run Tamara's setup doc to stand up your workspace and the habits.
2. Clone the Marketing OS into it. (I'll drop the link and the one-line setup once it's hosted.)
3. Point your CLAUDE.md at the brain so it loads every session.

That's it. Personal workspace, shared brain. Set yours up once, pull from the same source as everyone else, and give back to it as you learn.
