# How the two pieces fit together

You're getting two things from us this week. They stack, so you don't pick one.

**Your Workspace** is yours. Setting up your own Claude Code workspace gives you the folders, your CLAUDE.md, your memory, and the habits that keep it usable past month two. This is where you do your work, and it never leaves your machine.

**The Marketing OS** is the shared brain everyone works from: brand guidelines, ICP and segment sizing, positioning, the seven personas, voice rules, and our skills. One repo, one copy, everyone pulls from it.

Why two and not one: if we each keep our own copy of the brand guidelines, the day brand changes the palette, most of them are quietly wrong. The shared brain means we update once and everyone's next piece of work is current. It also gets smarter every time someone feeds a learning back in, which a pile of private folders never does.

It runs both ways. You **pull** from the brain constantly (copy, campaigns, the ICP, a persona, a skill). You **push** back to it when you learn something worth keeping, and the `share-learning` skill does the git for you, so you never touch a command line.

The restaurant version: your workspace is your own station. The Marketing OS is the walk-in the whole kitchen shares. You don't each buy your own tomatoes, and when you figure out a better prep, you put it back in the walk-in for everyone.

To get going:

1. Set up your own Claude Code workspace ([`set-up-your-workspace.md`](set-up-your-workspace.md)).
2. Set up the Marketing OS next to your workspace: unzip the copy Justin shared so you have `~/7shifts-marketing-os` (or `cd ~ && git clone https://github.com/justinholmes-7shifts/7shifts-marketing-os.git` if you have GitHub access).
3. Point your CLAUDE.md at the brain so it loads every session, and install the team skills (README step 3).

Your workspace, the shared brain. Set yours up once, pull from the same source, give back as you learn.
