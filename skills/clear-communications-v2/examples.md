# Clear Communications - Examples & Patterns

## Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Recommendation Email

**Before (Score: 2/5)**
> So I looked at the data from last quarter, and there were some interesting patterns in the West region. Sales were up 12% but costs were also higher. I talked to the regional managers and they mentioned the new incentive program. After analyzing everything, I think we should expand the incentive program to other regions.

**Problems:**
- Builds up to the point (bottom-up structure)
- Main message buried at the end
- No clear ask

**After (Score: 5/5)**
> We should expand the sales incentive program to all regions.
>
> The West region pilot increased sales 12% with positive ROI even after higher costs. Regional managers confirm it's driving behavior change.
>
> **Recommendation:** 3-region rollout in Q2. I need your approval by Friday to hit the timeline.

**What Changed:**
- Main message is first sentence
- Evidence follows logically
- Clear ask with deadline

### Example 2: Status Update

**Before (Score: 3/5)**
> Hi team, wanted to give you an update on the product launch. We've been working hard and made good progress. The design team finished the mockups. Engineering started coding. There are some concerns about the timeline though. Marketing needs more time for the campaign materials. We might need to talk about the launch date.

**Problems:**
- No clear status assessment upfront
- "Some concerns" is vague
- No explicit decision request

**After (Score: 5/5)**
> **We're 2 weeks behind on the product launch.** Marketing needs additional time for campaign materials.
>
> **Progress:**
> - Design: Complete (mockups approved)
> - Engineering: On track (60% complete)
> - Marketing: Behind (campaign materials delayed)
>
> **Decision Needed:** Should we push launch to March 15, or cut scope on marketing materials? I need direction by EOD Tuesday.

**What Changed:**
- Status assessment in first sentence (behind)
- Structured progress view
- Clear decision with options and deadline

---

### Example 3: Sharing Insights

**Before (Score: 2/5)**
> I've been looking at our customer data for the past few weeks. There's a lot of interesting stuff in there. Customers seem to have different behaviors based on when they signed up. The older customers use more features. New customers often get stuck on onboarding. I think there might be some opportunities here.

**Problems:**
- No main insight stated
- "Interesting stuff" is meaningless
- "Might be opportunities" is too vague

**After (Score: 5/5)**
> **New customers churn at 3x the rate of customers who've been with us 6+ months.** The root cause is onboarding friction.
>
> **Evidence:**
> - New customers (<3 mo): 15% monthly churn
> - Established customers (6+ mo): 5% monthly churn
> - 68% of churned new customers never completed onboarding
>
> **Recommendation:** Prioritize onboarding flow simplification in Q2. Expected impact: reduce new customer churn by 30%.

**What Changed:**
- Key insight is first sentence
- Impact is clear (3x churn rate)
- Evidence is specific and quantified
- Ends with recommendation

## Communication Patterns

### Recommendation Pattern
```
We should [action].

[Reason 1 with data]
[Reason 2 with data]
[Reason 3 with data]

Next steps: [specific plan]
Ask: [what you need from them]
```

### Status Update Pattern
```
We are [on track / behind / ahead] on [project].

Progress:
- [Area 1]: [status]
- [Area 2]: [status]

Risks:
- [Risk with mitigation]

Decision needed: [specific question with options]
```

### Problem + Solution Pattern
```
We have a problem: [X].

Root cause: [Y]

Solution: [Z]
- Step 1
- Step 2
- Step 3

Ask: [what you need]
```

### Insight Pattern
```
The key finding is [X].

This matters because [impact/implication].

Evidence:
- [Data point 1]
- [Data point 2]

Recommended action: [specific next step]
```

## Slide Title Test

**Weak titles (topic labels):**
- "Q1 Results"
- "Customer Feedback"
- "Project Timeline"

**Strong titles (assertions):**
- "Q1 Revenue Exceeded Target by 15%"
- "Customers Want Faster Onboarding, Not More Features"
- "We're 2 Weeks Behind Due to API Delays"

**Test:** If someone only read your slide titles, would they get the full story?

## Format-Specific Tips

| Format | Apply Pyramid Principle By... |
|--------|------------------------------|
| **Slack/Email** | First sentence = main message. Bold it. |
| **Slides** | Title = the point (assertion, not topic). Body = evidence. |
| **Loom/Video** | First 15 seconds = topic + key takeaway. |
| **Meeting** | Open with "Here's the main point..." |
| **Document** | Executive summary first. Details after. |

## Pre-Flight Checklist

Before sending any communication:

- [ ] What is my ONE main message? (One sentence)
- [ ] What do I want them to DO? (Decide/Approve/Be aware/Give input)
- [ ] What are my 2-4 supporting points? (Not 7. Not 10.)
- [ ] Have I cut everything that doesn't support the main message?
- [ ] Does my opening STATE the answer, not build up to it?
