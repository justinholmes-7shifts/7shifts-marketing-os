# Personas

> The real owners, directors, and managers we serve. Not theoretical. Each one wakes up knowing a single bad schedule or one payroll error can cost them money, staff, and their sanity. Pull these into any task that targets, segments, or writes to a buyer or user.
>
> We build exclusively for these people. That's the whole differentiator: horizontal tools (Homebase) and bundled POS features (Toast) target everyone and serve no one. Map each persona to a segment in [`icp.md`](icp.md).

**Buying roles:** Economic buyer = Owner/Operator (Chloe, Olivia, George, Michelle). Champion/influencer = Director or GM (Daniel, Mike, Jason). End user = hourly staff. A tool has to win the buyer on ROI and the user on daily ease, or adoption dies.

## 1. Chloe, the "Time-Starved" Independent Owner

Owner/operator of a single-location independent (25 employees, open 5 years). Her passion project, but she's trapped in it: chief bookkeeper, HR, and scheduler. Every minute in the back office is a minute off the floor and a dollar off the bottom line.

- **Daily reality:** hand-keying payroll from a binder into QuickBooks; shift-swap texts clogging her personal phone; fighting Excel to build the schedule; surprise overtime; covering a no-show while running payroll.
- **Fears:** burnout; losing a great server to a schedule mistake; overtime wiping out the week's profit; missing payroll and bouncing checks.
- **Motivations:** get 10 hours/week back; know labor cost before a shift starts; a calm, stable system; finally take a real vacation.
- **Trust builders:** "Show me it integrates with my POS." "Prove it stops early clock-ins." "Let me try it free, no credit card, no sales call." "Show me payroll in 10 minutes, not 2 hours."
- **Trust killers:** clunky/slow mobile app; hidden or enterprise pricing; generic "small business" software; being forced to talk to sales for one location.
- **Core pain:** 6+ hrs/week on schedules and payroll; no true labor cost until after payroll runs; staff clocking in 5-10 min early; time-off buried in texts; re-keying punches between systems.

## 2. Mike, the "Spreadsheet-Struggling" GM

Salaried GM at an independent like Chloe's (20-30 hourly staff). A people person, 10+ years in, loves the floor, drowns in back-office admin. Works 55 hours/week and feels half of it is spent managing the schedule instead of the restaurant.

- **Daily reality:** updating the master Excel for the 5th time this week; fielding angry shift-swap calls; checking availability in a binder; training a host while covering the bar; getting grilled about last week's labor.
- **Fears:** a critical gap on a Friday; staff anger over unfair schedules; losing a good server next door; the owner blaming him for labor he can't control.
- **Motivations:** a fair schedule built in 30 minutes; a happy stable team; getting out of the shift-swap middleman role; one simple tool that makes him look good.
- **Trust builders:** "Drag-and-drop template that builds my schedule in one click." "An app my staff actually like." "Swaps without me as the bottleneck." "In-app chat so I can drop WhatsApp."
- **Trust killers:** complicated/ugly/slow UI; manual availability entry; crashes and logouts; enterprise features he doesn't need; calling staff for approvals.
- **Core pain:** scheduling takes hours across Excel + notebook; he's the bottleneck for every swap; staff miss texted updates; no labor visibility until too late; he feels like an admin assistant, not a GM.

## 3. Olivia, the "Growth-Focused" Multi-Unit Owner

Owns a growing group (4 locations, 80+ employees), scaling toward 10. Was hands-on like Chloe, now forced into full-time Owner/CFO. Already uses a scheduling system. Lack of standardized systems and data is creating chaos and capping her growth.

- **Daily reality:** chasing 4 GMs for labor reports; merging 4 Excel payroll files; reading P&Ls formatted 4 different ways; spotting one store paying 5% more labor for the same sales; driving between locations.
- **Fears:** inconsistent service hurting the brand; losing financial control; no real-time aggregated labor cost; a rogue GM running up unapproved overtime.
- **Motivations:** standardize to scale 4→10 confidently; all locations' labor-vs-sales on one dashboard; trust GMs follow the plan; a repeatable, profitable model.
- **Trust builders:** "One dashboard comparing labor % vs sales across all stores." "Aggregate payroll from 4 locations into one clean file." "Set and enforce GM rules." "Deep integration with my 3 POS systems."
- **Trust killers:** manual data merging; a single-location tool pretending to be multi-unit; no GM-vs-Director permissions; generic reports (she needs store-vs-store); payroll failures (her biggest risk).
- **Core pain:** 5-hour manual payroll aggregation every two weeks; can't compare labor across differently-formatted reports; zero real-time store visibility; can only "remind" GMs of budgets, not enforce; hard to manage staff across locations; chaotic GM onboarding; flying blind in front of investors.

## 4. Daniel, the "Process & Enforcement" Director

New Director of Operations for Olivia's 4-location group. The role was just created because Olivia can't be everywhere. A systems thinker responsible for making every GM follow the company playbook on scheduling, training, labor, and compliance.

- **Daily reality:** auditing 4 GMs' schedules for OT/compliance; emailing the 28% labor-target reminder again; coordinating a server across 2 locations; remotely reading Manager Log Books; training an AGM the "company way"; building a standardized onboarding checklist.
- **Fears:** GMs doing their own thing; a compliance issue (missed breaks) blowing up; being seen as the "bad cop" / micromanager; fighting fires instead of building systems.
- **Motivations:** one standardized operating playbook; help GMs succeed with better tools and clear rules; audit performance without driving to every store; prove his value to Olivia with group-wide results.
- **Trust builders:** "See and manage staff across all locations in one view." "A central digital log book to audit shift notes remotely." "Enforce labor rules and permissions, not just suggest them." "Templates I can push to all locations."
- **Trust killers:** no consolidated multi-location view; a separate report per store; no audit trail for manager changes; staff-level features without oversight; a UI GMs find clunky (kills adoption).
- **Core pain:** manually nagging GMs on targets and compliance; multi-location staff scheduling nightmare; slow manager training because every store has its own method; can't see shift notes remotely; can't enforce standard roles group-wide; "nagging, not leading"; can't prove to Olivia who's following the rules.

## 5. George, the "Compliance-Obsessed" QSR Franchisee

Owns 8 QSR franchise units (think Quiznos, Panera, Jamba). 100+ employees, very high turnover, high-volume low-complexity ops. His model lives or dies on speed, replication, and 100% adherence to the franchisor's rules and state labor law. More a "system" person than a "restaurant" person.

- **Daily reality:** reviewing mandated-POS sales data; prepping for a surprise corporate audit; checking minors' work-hour rules; trying to make POS + scheduler + payroll talk; rolling out a corporate promo; 30% quarterly turnover.
- **Fears:** failing a compliance audit; a fine/lawsuit over a break-law violation; losing the franchise license; GMs not using the approved labor model.
- **Motivations:** a high franchisor compliance score; standardize labor across 8 units with one template; perfect automated schedules from sales data; onboard a manager in one day.
- **Trust builders:** "Prove you integrate with my franchisor's mandated POS." "Built-in customizable compliance rules for my state and franchise." "Copy a standardized schedule template to all 8 units." "Simple enough for a 20-year-old shift lead."
- **Trust killers:** "my POS isn't on your list"; customization (he wants standardization); system silos; any tool that allows deviation from the corporate model; complex FSR features (tip pooling) cluttering the UI.
- **Core pain:** terror of a missed mandated break triggering a fine; siloed systems forcing manual entry; can't easily prove compliance to corporate; slow GM onboarding; can't auto-forecast from POS sales; high-risk manual management of minors' hours.

## 6. Michelle, the "High-Volume" FSR Owner/CFO  ⭐ Best Fit

Owner/CFO of 3 high-volume full-service restaurants (40-50+ employees each). Defined by complexity: high sales, overlapping roles (servers, bussers, runners, hosts), brutal tip-out rules. Her nightmare is bi-weekly payroll, specifically tip distribution and overtime. **This is the segment we prioritize.**

- **Daily reality:** her GM (Jason) hand-calculating tip pools in Excel; surprise overtime in the thousands; servers saying their tip-out was wrong again; getting a clean payroll file to her accountant; turnover from payroll disputes; auditing punches vs server sales.
- **Fears:** a wage-and-hour lawsuit over tips; disputes and turnover from payroll errors; unnoticed overtime bleeding the business; losing control as staff volume grows.
- **Motivations:** fully automate complex tip pooling (zero human error); real-time overtime alerts before they hit; a transparent, trusted payroll system; tight real-time financial control.
- **Trust builders:** "Show me exactly how you handle my 5% server-to-runner, 2% runner-to-kitchen pool." "Prove your POS pulls the exact sales and role data for tips." "Real-time OT alerts across all 3 locations." "How you log and resolve payroll/tip disputes."
- **Trust killers:** "basic" tip pooling (split by hours is useless to her); any report of payroll/calculation failures; OT reporting that's only after the week ends; generic QSR features; being told to change her tip-out rules to fit the software.
- **Core pain:** 4-hour weekly manual tip calc prone to costly error; constant morale-killing disputes; OT caught too late; lost control of an always-changing 40+ schedule; high-risk multi-location payroll; GMs burning out on admin; can't trust her labor data.

## 7. Jason, the "High-Turnover" Full-Service GM  ⭐ Best Fit

GM of one of Michelle's high-volume 40-employee restaurants. Lives in constant change. The schedule is never "finished," and he spends a huge chunk of his week investigating and defending payroll and tip math to staff.

- **Daily reality:** 10+ swap approvals before 10am; a server disputing last week's hours or tips; hand-calculating a multi-role tip pool in a spreadsheet; a schedule in constant flux; chasing a no-show bartender replacement; explaining high OT to Michelle.
- **Fears:** a server accusing him of stealing tips over a spreadsheet error; the schedule collapsing on a busy Saturday; losing his best server over a $20 dispute; Michelle seeing him as unable to control team or costs.
- **Motivations:** let staff manage their own swaps; a trusted, automated, transparent tip system so staff stop blaming him; an auditable dispute log; time on the floor coaching, not on a spreadsheet.
- **Trust builders:** "Give staff a Shift Marketplace to handle their own swaps." "A simple digital log for time-clock and tip disputes." "Prove tip calcs are 100% accurate and transparent to staff." "An app 40+ people can use without pestering me."
- **Trust killers:** a "basic" swap feature that still needs his approval for everything; any manual tip double-checking; an app that doesn't clearly show staff their tips and hours; a slow/buggy time clock; digging through emails or paper to resolve a dispute.
- **Core pain:** constant chaotic schedule flux; hours as the swap middleman; being the "bad guy" on hour/tip disputes; messy paper dispute logs; no defensible way to handle complex tip-outs; burnout; can't approve swaps fast enough so people no-show.

## How we serve them

We don't just serve these personas, we're built exclusively for them. The restaurant-only focus solves their high-friction problems in ways horizontal competitors and bundled POS features can't.

- **Chloe + Mike:** kill the spreadsheet struggle, save 6+ hrs/week. Simple templates, time clocking that stops early punch-ins, a Shift Marketplace that ends Mike's middleman role. Speaks to burnout and getting back on the floor.
- **Olivia + Daniel:** solve data inconsistency and enforcement fatigue. An Operations Overview dashboard (Olivia's single pane of glass), multi-location permissions and the Manager Log Book (Daniel's remote audit + enforcement). A scalable, standardized playbook.
- **George:** the compliance engine. Customizable compliance tools that auto-track breaks and minors' hours; standardized role templates to push the corporate labor model to all units. De-risks the operation.
- **Michelle + Jason:** their biggest nightmare, tip-pooling errors. Advanced Tip Pooling built for FSR complexity, plus Overtime Alerts. Addresses Michelle's lawsuit fear and Jason's dispute fear, rebuilding the trust manual errors destroyed.
