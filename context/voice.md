# 7shifts Brand Voice

> How the 7shifts marketing team sounds. This is the **company** voice, shared by everyone, and the canonical home for verbal voice (the brand-guidelines doc points here). It is distinct from any individual's personal writing voice. Justin's `justin-voice` skill is his, for his own posts and memos. This is 7shifts'.
>
> Pull this into any human-facing writing: campaigns, emails, landing pages, social, readouts. Seeded from the Winter 2024 brand system (Fuzzco). Add to it as we learn what lands.

## Voice (never changes)

**Knowledgeable. Clear. Warm. Helpful.**

We sound like people who actually understand the restaurant business, not a tech company looking in from the outside. On your side, by your side.

## The one rule above all others

**The operator and the staff are the hero. 7shifts is the trustworthy sidekick.** Lead with their outcome, never our feature list. If a sentence makes 7shifts the hero, rewrite it.

## Tone (shifts by audience and context)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| To **employees** | Playful, conversational | "Knives down: it's break time." |
| To **operators, in-product** | Straightforward, action-oriented | "Attention needed. Review and resolve the scheduling conflicts below." |
| To **operators, in marketing** | Confident | "Easy to use, easier to love. Make your first schedule for free." |
| About **ourselves** | Reassuring, clear-cut | "We're here to support your entire restaurant team." |

When you don't know which audience a piece is for, ask. The tone shifts meaningfully between operators and employees.

## Calibration: "we always sound clear"

Find the middle. Not vague, not clipped.

- Not enough: "Improve your restaurant operations with better labor cost management."
- **Just right: "Save time and money with team scheduling and payroll."**
- Too much: "Schedule and pay your team."

## Sanctioned statements (use these, or write in their key)

- Great teams make great restaurants
- Building successful restaurants, one shift at a time
- The all-in-one app behind strong restaurant teams
- You support your team, we support you
- Restaurants are chaotic, 7shifts isn't
- We just 86'd kitchen commotion
- People-powered, every step of the way

## Restaurant vocabulary

On-brand and welcome: 86, knives down, last call, tip-out, FOH/BOH, shift happens, in the weeds. **One per piece, not three.** Overdoing it reads like a costume.

## Never write

- Generic SaaS speak: "optimize workflows," "leverage synergies," "transform your business"
- Corporate hedges: "we strive to," "we aim to"
- Anything that talks down to a hospitality worker
- AI tells: "in today's fast-paced world," "seamless," "revolutionary," "game-changing"
- Em dashes

## Examples (before / after)

Collect these as we go. This is how the voice becomes teachable.

| Off-voice | On-voice |
|---|---|
| "Leverage our platform to optimize labor spend." | "Spend less on labor without overthinking it." |
| "7shifts transforms restaurant operations." | "Restaurants are chaotic, 7shifts isn't." |
